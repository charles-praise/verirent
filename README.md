#  VERIRENT
# Nigeria's First Professional Agency Certification Marketplace
# "Rent with Certainty."

VeriRent NG is Nigeria's first professional certification marketplace for rental property. Unlike listing
aggregators or traditional agency platforms, VeriRent NG does not list properties — it certifies the
registered agencies that do. Every agency on the platform holds active ESVARBON registration, has
undergone a rigorous platform compliance audit, and operates under a contractual standard higher than
any existing Nigerian PropTech platform enforces.

## Problem Statement
Nigeria's rental sector — particularly in Port Harcourt — is characterised by systemic inefficiencies and
trust deficits that impose substantial costs on all market participants. With Port Harcourt ranking among
Nigeria's most expensive rental markets due to sustained oil and gas sector employment, the stakes of
these failures are exceptionally high.

* Fraud & Title Disputes
An estimated 30–40% of informal property listings in Port Harcourt involve unverified or encumbered titles,
exposing tenants to fake landlords, double-letting, and outright financial loss. Legal disputes arising from
these transactions can take years to resolve and routinely cost more than the rental deposit at stake.

* Excessive & Opaque Fees
Traditional agency commissions of 10–15% of annual rent — compounded by multiple inspection fees,
agreement fees, and caution deposits — impose significant upfront costs without transparent justification or
commensurate professional value. VeriRent NG's all-in fee structure is materially lower and fully disclosed
upfront.

* Fragmented & Unaccountable Agency Standards
Rivers State hosts hundreds of estate agents operating to wildly inconsistent quality standards. Landlords
cannot distinguish a qualified ESVARBON-registered surveyor from an unlicensed tout. Tenants bear the full
cost of this information gap. VeriRent NG makes the distinction visible, enforceable, and commercially
advantageous for compliant agencies.

* Communication Breakdown & Dispute Escalation
Maintenance requests go unacknowledged for weeks; rent remittances are unreceipted; breach notices are
issued informally or not at all. The result is costly litigation and strained landlord-tenant relationships that
erode the entire market's efficiency.

* Information Asymmetry for High-Value Tenants
Oil and gas professionals, corporate relocatees, and diaspora investors lack the time or local knowledge to
efficiently identify legitimate, quality accommodation. Corporate HR departments report that staff relocations
take 3–4 weeks on average due to title disputes — a direct operational cost to their businesses
